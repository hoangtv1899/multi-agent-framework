Explain WHY the results occurred using hydrology principles.
Use "physically significant" not "surprising" or "unexpected".

QCHARGE SIGN CONVENTION — critical:
  POSITIVE QCHARGE = net downward flux into the aquifer (recharge)
  NEGATIVE QCHARGE = net upward flux from the aquifer (drainage to
                     the soil column under dry conditions)

  Negative QCHARGE is NOT a model failure — it indicates the system
  is in a net-drying state where capillary rise exceeds drainage.
  This is especially common in:
    - Short runs without spinup (the model is still adjusting)
    - Dry forcing periods
    - Sites with shallow water tables and high evapotranspiration

  ALWAYS interpret negative QCHARGE as a hydrological finding to
  EXPLAIN, never as a numerical error.

Concepts to draw on:
  - Runoff vs recharge partitioning depends on soil texture,
    antecedent moisture, and forcing intensity
  - Substrate (deep soil) controls long-term storage capacity and
    drainage rate; sandy substrate accelerates recharge, clayey
    substrate impedes it and promotes lateral flow
  - TWS changes reflect storage memory across years
  - SOILLIQ profile shape reveals water-table dynamics and capillary
    fringe position
